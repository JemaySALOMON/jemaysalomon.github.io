About

Jemay Salomon

QUANTITATIVE GENETICS . STATISTICAL GENETICS . PLANT BREEDING . MIXED MODELS

I am a quantitative genetics researcher focused on developing statistical and computational tools to dissect the genetic architecture of complex traits and to optimize plant breeding programs. My work sits at the intersection of mixed model theory, stochastic simulation, and tools development — translating methodological advances into practical, scalable breeding strategies.

My research interests span genomic prediction and selection, variance component estimation, simulation-based optimization of multi-stage breeding schemes, and the quantitative genetics of complex traits — including inter-genotype competition and selection response in heterogeneous groups.
